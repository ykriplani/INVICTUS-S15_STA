ABNROMAL UTERINE BLEEDING

Overview

Abnormal uterine bleeding (formerly called menometrorrhagia) is when you bleed between your monthly periods or when your periods are extremely heavy and/or prolonged. Normal menstrual flow typically lasts about five days and occurs every 21 to 35 days. Your provider should know about any abnormal bleeding you’re experiencing. What’s causing your bleeding may be harmless. But your bleeding may be a sign of cancer or conditions that may negatively impact your fertility.

Symtoms

1. Heavy menstrual bleeding (formerly called menorrhagia).
2. Bleeding at unusual times (between periods, after intercourse).
3. Unusually long periods (seven days or longer).
4. Inconsistent menstrual cycles.

Causes

1. Hormone imbalances
2. Anovulation.
3. Thyroid disease.
4. Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).
5. Weighing more than your ideal body weight can lead to hormone imbalances that may cause abnormal uterine bleeding, too.
6. Structural abnormalities in your uterus
7. Polyps.
8. Fibroids.
9. Precancer and cancer.
10. Uterine cancer.
11. Cervical cancer.
12.Vaginal cancer.
13. Ovarian cancer.

Complications

1. Trouble getting pregnant
2. Anaemia or blood loss
3. Higher risk of endometrial cancer
